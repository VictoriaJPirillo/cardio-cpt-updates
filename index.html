Introduction

In 2015, our team uncovered a slew of billing denials tied to subcutaneous implantable cardioverter-defibrillator (S-ICD) device codes. Despite my main role focusing on facesheet data entry and follow-up scheduling, I noticed a peculiar pattern of rejections. One routine cross-training session led me into an investigative deep dive of CPT code updates, documentation reviews, and a bold initiative in revenue cycle recovery.

This page outlines:

  • A personal account of how we discovered the code discrepancy.
  • A brief on the 2015 cardiology coding changes.
  • Insights into preventing overlooked coding errors.
  • Actionable tips and checklists for healthcare coders and staff.

Fun Fact: Why It's So Important

Subcutaneous ICDs (S-ICDs) are a newer approach to treat patients at risk for sudden cardiac death. Because of the specialized and precise nature of these devices, accurate coding is vital to ensure proper tracking of outcomes and reimbursement. A single wrongly assigned code can ripple through months of billing and potentially cost thousands.
